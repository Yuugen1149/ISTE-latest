ABOUT ISTE

Indian Society for Technical Education

History & Mission

The Indian Society for Technical Education (ISTE) is a national, professional, non-profit organization of educators in engineering and technology. It originated as the Association of Principals of Technical Institutions (APTI) in 1941 at Delhi Polytechnic and was renamed ISTE on 21 May 1968, expanding its scope to include all stakeholders in technical education. Headquartered in New Delhi, ISTE is dedicated to improving the quality and standards of technical education in India.

Institutional Chapters

The society has a vast network with over 100,000 faculty members, 500,000+ student members, and 2,000+ institutional chapters across India. It operates through state sections, faculty chapters, and student chapters, coordinated by a National Executive Council.

Major Activities

  • Faculty Development Programs
  • Seminars and Workshops
  • Student Conventions
  • Technical Paper Contests
  • Curriculum Innovation
  • Industry-Academia Collaboration
  • Awards, Fellowships, and Scholarships

Collaboration & Impact

Working closely with AICTE, NBA, and other government bodies, ISTE plays a vital role in policy-making and academic reform. It provides a platform for teachers and students to exchange ideas, enhance skills, and adapt to emerging technologies such as AI, IoT, and data science.

Legacy

In essence, ISTE is the oldest and largest professional body advancing technical education in India through innovation, collaboration, and continuous improvement.
